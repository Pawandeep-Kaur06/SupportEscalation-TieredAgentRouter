TITLE:
Phishing Recognition and Response

CATEGORY:
Security

PROBLEM:
- Receiving suspicious emails, text messages, or direct messages attempting to solicit personal information.
- Risk of inadvertently falling for a phishing attack leading to account compromise or data loss.

COMMON CAUSES:
- Urgent call to action or threats.
- Messages from first-time, infrequent, or external senders.
- Spelling and bad grammar.
- Generic greetings (e.g., "Dear sir or madam").
- Mismatched email domains or subtle misspellings in sender address.
- Outlook banners indicating sender verification failure.
- Suspicious links or unexpected attachments.

TROUBLESHOOTING STEPS:
1. Pause; examine messages for urgency, grammar, generic greetings, domain mismatches, or Outlook banners.
2. Hover over links (or long-press on mobile) to verify destination; do not click suspicious links or open attachments.
3. If suspicious, do not respond; instead, navigate to the organization's official website or call official numbers.
4. Report suspicious messages: Use Outlook/Teams reporting tools or forward as attachment to phish@office365.microsoft.com.
5. If phished: document details, immediately change affected passwords (strong, unique), enable MFA.
6. Notify IT support (work/school accounts), contact banks/credit card companies if financial info shared.

RESOLUTION TIME:
- Immediate

ESCALATION CONDITION:
- User has clicked a suspicious link or opened an attachment.
- User has entered personal information, credentials, or financial details.
- User suspects their account has been compromised or data leaked.

ESCALATE TO:
- Help Desk Team

PRIORITY:
- Critical
